TEN SONGS ON A SUNDAY AFTERNOON (PART TWO)

witchcraft, white one you said, tongues of fire trying to lick the sky
filling the blanks in your soul, immortal soul
the first one, he died like a soldier during peace time
the second like a priest at a funeral party

how many more before you put yourself in line for the bread
instead of eating hyacinths and playing dragonflies and tigerlillies

bitchcraft, nice one, i say, tongues of a wet mouth
trying to wash your body, immortal body
i won't be the third

humble me, let me ask for what i need: what i need is goodbye
let me quench my thirst on your chalice

humble me, let me accept what i want: for what i want is goodbye
let me quench my thirst on your chalice

humble me, let me be sure of what i crave: what i crave is goodbye
humble me, remove this cup from me so we can toast our farewell 

it is saturday tonight: i don’t know why, i really think i shouldn’t
but i am thinking about you
you know it is the same scene, and suddenly aim back at the lagoon
drinking beer, missing you, the perfume you know, is the same, don’t you remember?

i used to love the way you smelt on a saturday like today
it is hard to accept you are nothing but the past
i can’t pretend anymore, you are nothing but the past
cover me with kisses babe, kiss me with lust
i man sure we will never lust again

worms writhe underground, looking for something to drink
but found instead something that glows in the dark
the silkworm fell on his back from jacob's ladder
woke up, counted down from fifty to the day we last met

yawned away the most lucid dream, deciding to do what makes you happy
then raced to northern seashores with scarlett superstrong
silkworm tells me: was this the miracle? the tiny miracle?
we were waiting for, for so long?

autem [ cæca | olor | custodia ] magnique
